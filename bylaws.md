# By-Laws of Civic Tech Atlanta, Inc.

---

## Article I: Offices

### Section 1.1 - Registered Office and Agent

The corporation shall maintain a registered office in the State of Georgia with a registered agent whose address matches the office location, as required by the Georgia Nonprofit Corporation Code.

### Section 1.2 - Other Offices

The organization may establish additional offices at such place or places within or without the State of Georgia as the Board of Directors may determine or as the business of the Corporation may require.

---

## Article II: Nature and Purpose of the Corporation

### Section 2.1 - Nonprofit Corporation

The Corporation operates under Georgia Nonprofit Corporation Code § 14-3-100 et seq. As a nonprofit entity without capital stock or shareholders, no part of the net earnings, income, or profit of the Corporation shall inure to the benefit of or be distributable to its directors, officers, or other private individual, except for reasonable compensation and charitable distributions.

### Section 2.2 - Purposes

#### 2.2.1 Section 501(c)(3) Status

The organization is established for charitable and educational purposes qualifying under Internal Revenue Code Section 501(c)(3), restricting activities to those meeting this tax-exempt classification.

#### 2.2.2 Primary Purpose

The Corporation exists for the purpose of organizing our community to work on civic-minded, social good projects via technology that make metro Atlanta more inclusive, diverse, equitable, and accessible.

#### 2.2.3 Incidental Powers

The Board may undertake actions necessary or helpful to accomplish the Corporation's objectives, exercising authority permitted under Georgia law while respecting IRS restrictions.

#### 2.2.4 Annual Project List

The Board must review and approve specific projects at each annual meeting before requesting community support or funding.

### Section 2.3 - Governing Instruments

The Articles of Incorporation and these By-Laws provide the Corporation's governance framework.

---

## Article III: Directors

### Section 3.1 - Powers

The Board of Directors is responsible as a body for overall policy and governance and may exercise all lawful powers not restricted by law or the Articles of Incorporation.

### Section 3.2 - Number of Directors

The board comprises 3-10 members. The board may adjust the precise number within these limits, though reductions take effect only upon member departure or term expiration.

### Section 3.3 - Vacancies

Remaining directors fill vacancies through nomination and majority vote at a properly noticed meeting, provided nominations are submitted to the Board President at least two weeks in advance.

### Section 3.4 - Manner of Election

Directors are elected by majority vote of sitting directors when a quorum is present, taking office upon meeting conclusion.

### Section 3.5 - One Year Exception Period

Directors may serve four consecutive one-year terms. After completing four terms, they must take a mandatory one-year leave, unless the board waives this requirement under special and compelling circumstances.

### Section 3.6 - Directors' Meetings

The board meets at least quarterly with two weeks' notice. Meetings may occur anywhere within or outside Georgia.

### Section 3.7 - Compensation

Directors receive no compensation, though the board may authorize reasonable expense reimbursement by resolution or policy.

### Section 3.8 - Absences and Termination

The board may suspend or terminate directors for good cause with 15 days' written notice and an opportunity to be heard. Missing more than three meetings annually may trigger removal proceedings. A three-quarters supermajority vote is required for expulsion.

### Section 3.9 - Vacancies

Vacancies are filled by majority vote of remaining directors for the unexpired term.

### Section 3.10 - Resignations

Board members resigning before term completion must submit written notice to the Board President.

### Section 3.11 - Conflict of Interest Policy

The board establishes, reviews, and annually signs a conflict of interest policy.

---

## Article IV: Committees

### Section 4.1 - Committee Creation and Authority

The Board of Directors may establish committees by majority vote. Committees are to consist of one or more of the directors of the Corporation and can exercise powers delegated by the Board in managing organizational affairs.

- Committees receive names reflecting their intended purpose
- The Board determines committee powers and responsibilities
- The Board may dissolve committees by simple majority vote once their objectives are fulfilled

### Section 4.2 - Committee Procedures

The procedures governing Board meetings—including notice requirements, quorum standards, voting protocols, and telephonic participation—extend to all committees and their members.

---

## Article V: Meetings of the Board of Directors

### Section 5.1 - Annual Meetings

The organization may hold annual board meetings on the last business day of the fourth month after the fiscal year closes, or at another time determined by the board. The purpose is electing directors and officers.

### Section 5.2 - Regular Meetings

The board conducts regular meetings with at least two weeks' notice, held within or outside Georgia as determined by directors. At a minimum, the Board of Directors will meet quarterly.

### Section 5.3 - Special or Emergency Meetings

The President can call special meetings with one day's notice via mail, email, or personal delivery. Any two or more directors may also request such meetings. Directors may waive notice by signing a document or attending the meeting.

### Section 5.4 - Notice

Meeting notices need not specify purposes unless law requires it.

### Section 5.5 - Quorum

A majority of board members present constitutes a quorum. The vote of a majority of the Board present shall be an act when quorum exists, unless greater approval is needed.

### Section 5.6 - Action Without a Meeting

Directors may act through unanimous written consent via signed documents or email, provided the communication comes directly from the director and clearly states consent to the proposed action.

### Section 5.7 - Telephone and Similar Meetings

Directors may participate through conference calls or similar technology, with participation counting as physical attendance.

---

## Article VI: Officers

### Section 6.1 - Officer Positions

The Board elects a President (CEO), Vice-President(s), Executive Director, Secretary, and Treasurer (CFO), with authority to create additional roles as needed.

### Section 6.2 - Election and Terms

Officers are elected annually during board meetings through nomination by peers or self-nomination. Each serves until their successor qualifies or they resign or are removed.

### Section 6.3 - President

The President serves as ex officio committee member, chairs board meetings, and exercises powers assigned by the Board.

### Section 6.4 - Vice-President

The Vice-President performs typical VP duties and other tasks delegated by the President or Board.

### Section 6.5 - Secretary

The Secretary records minutes, maintains official records, provides meeting notices, and performs Board-assigned duties.

### Section 6.6 - Treasurer

The Treasurer manages all funds, securities, and financial accounts; reports financial status; and heads the Finance Committee if established.

### Section 6.7 - Executive Director

The Executive Director handles day-to-day operations, executes Board policies, attends meetings, and reports progress. The Executive Director can be terminated by simple majority vote.

### Section 6.8 - Restrictions

One person cannot simultaneously hold both President and Secretary positions, though individuals may hold up to two offices otherwise.

### Section 6.9 - Compensation

Board members receive no salary for service but may be reimbursed for expenses. The Board may authorize reasonable compensation for officers serving as employees.

---

## Article VII: Miscellaneous

### Section 7.1 - Books and Records

The Board may determine which corporate accounts and books are open to inspection and set reasonable rules for access, consistent with applicable law.

### Section 7.2 - Depositories

Corporate funds are deposited in designated financial institutions and withdrawn via authorized signatures as determined by the Board.

### Section 7.3 - Legal Instruments

The President or other Board-designated representatives sign contracts and official documents on behalf of the organization.

### Section 7.4 - Fiscal Year

The Board establishes the Corporation's fiscal year through resolution.

### Section 7.5 - Seal

The corporate seal's form is determined by the Board.

### Section 7.6 - Annual Statements

Within four months after each fiscal year closes, the Corporation prepares a balance sheet and profit/loss statement detailing its financial condition and operational results.

### Section 7.7 - Indemnification

The organization indemnifies directors and officers to the maximum extent permitted by Georgia law and the Internal Revenue Code when they act in good faith and reasonably believe their conduct served the organization's interests. The Corporation may advance reasonable legal expenses with written affirmations of good faith and undertakings for repayment if indemnification is ultimately denied.

### Section 7.8 - Insurance

The Corporation may purchase insurance protecting directors, officers, and agents against expenses and liabilities.

### Section 7.9 - Incidental Expenses

Officers and directors personally bear incidental expenses unless specifically authorized for reimbursement.

---

## Article VIII: Notices; Waivers of Notice

### Section 8.1 - Notice Requirements

The organization may provide notice to directors or officers through multiple methods: in-person delivery, telephone, electronic transmission, mail, or private carrier. Notice is considered delivered when sent or mailed to the address listed in the corporation's records.

### Section 8.2 - Waiver of Notice

Directors and officers may waive required notice in writing, using the same delivery methods listed above. The waiver can occur before or after the meeting time. Additionally, attendance at a meeting itself constitutes an implicit waiver of notice requirements.

---

## Article IX: Amendments and Review

### Section 9.1 - Amendment Process

The board may alter existing by-laws or create new ones through a super-majority, defined as a three-quarters majority vote at any board meeting. Proposed changes require submission to the Secretary no fewer than two weeks before a scheduled meeting, with board members receiving materials at least one week prior.

### Section 9.2 - Periodic Review

The board must conduct a comprehensive review of these by-laws at minimum once every three years. If no modifications are needed, the Secretary documents that the review occurred without requiring a formal vote.

### Section 9.3 - Record Retention

Previous versions of by-laws existing before any amendments are preserved for seven years and remain accessible to all board members for inspection purposes.
